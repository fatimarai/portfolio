Research Case Study

Comparison of Ovarian Volume in Fertile and Infertile Married Females

A transabdominal ultrasound-based comparative study evaluating ovarian volume differences between fertile and infertile married females.

BMIS Research
Pelvic Modality
Comp. Study

This research case study focused on comparing ovarian volume between fertile and infertile married females using transabdominal ultrasound imaging. The objective was to assess whether ovarian volume measurements could serve as a useful sonographic marker in evaluating female infertility.

The study involved careful patient preparation, optimal transducer selection, and standardized measurement techniques for ovarian length, width, and depth — applying the ellipsoid formula to calculate ovarian volume.
